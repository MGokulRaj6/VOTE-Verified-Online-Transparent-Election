Voting Procedure

A step-by-step guide to the democratic process of casting your vote in Indian elections.

01

Register as a Voter

Eligible citizens must register with the Election Commission and obtain a valid Voter ID (EPIC card) to participate in elections.

02

Locate Your Polling Station

Find your designated polling booth using the Election Commission portal or the Voter Helpline app by entering your EPIC number.

03

Identity Verification

On election day, visit your polling station with a valid photo ID. Officials verify your identity against the electoral roll.

04

Cast Your Vote

Enter the voting booth, use the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) to cast your secret ballot, and collect the VVPAT slip for verification.

05

Indelible Ink Mark

After voting, your finger is marked with indelible ink to prevent duplicate voting. The ink lasts for several days.

Importance of Voting

Understanding why every vote matters in building a stronger, more equitable nation.

Foundation of Democracy

Voting is the cornerstone of any democracy. It gives citizens the power to choose their leaders and influence government policies that affect their daily lives.

Voice of the People

Every single vote counts. Your vote is your voice in deciding who represents you, how public funds are spent, and what direction your community takes.

Accountability & Governance

When citizens actively vote, elected officials are held accountable. Higher voter turnout leads to better governance and more responsive leaders.

Social Responsibility

Voting is both a right and a duty. Citizens who choose not to vote risk allowing others to determine their future. Be the change you wish to see.

Fake Vote Awareness

Understand common threats to electoral integrity and how the system guards against them.

Bogus Voting / Booth Capturing

Forcibly taking control of a polling station and casting fake votes is a serious criminal offence. Modern EVMs and CCTV surveillance have made this increasingly difficult.

Impersonation

Voting in someone else's name is voter fraud. The Election Commission uses photo ID verification, indelible ink, and biometric checks to prevent impersonation.

Duplicate Voter Registration

Registering under multiple names or at multiple addresses to cast more than one vote is illegal. The EPIC database is cross-verified to detect duplicates.

Vote Buying & Bribery

Offering or accepting money, gifts, or favours in exchange for votes is a criminal offence. Report such activities to the Election Commission immediately.

Spreading Misinformation

Circulating false information about candidates, polling dates, or voting procedures on social media or otherwise undermines democracy and may be punishable by law.

Punishment for Fake Voting

Legal consequences under Indian law for electoral fraud and malpractice.

Section 171F

Undue Influence at Elections

Up to 1 year imprisonment or fine, or both

Any voluntary interference or attempt to interfere with the free exercise of electoral right is punishable under the Indian Penal Code.

Section 171C

Bribery in Elections

Up to 1 year imprisonment or fine, or both

Giving or accepting any gratification as a motive or reward for voting or refraining from voting is a criminal offence.

Section 135A

Booth Capturing

Minimum 1 year, up to 3 years imprisonment + fine

Seizing a polling station, taking possession of EVMs, or threatening voters and polling officers is severely punishable under the RP Act, 1951.

Section 171D

Personation at Elections

Up to 1 year imprisonment or fine, or both

Applying for a ballot paper or voting in the name of another person, whether living, dead, or fictitious, is an offence under the IPC.
